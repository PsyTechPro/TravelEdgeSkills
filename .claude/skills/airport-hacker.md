## Lounge Access — The Complete Guide

### Priority Pass (Most Important)
Priority Pass is the largest independent lounge network — 1,400+ lounges in 600+ airports worldwide. Access comes through credit cards:

**Cards that include Priority Pass (Select — unlimited visits):**
- Chase Sapphire Reserve ($550/year) — you + guests free
- Amex Platinum ($695/year) — you only, guests $50 each (or Centurion Lounges which are much better)
- Citi Prestige ($495/year)
- Capital One Venture X ($395/year) — you + 2 guests free (best value lounge card)

**Cards with limited Priority Pass:**
- Chase Sapphire Preferred — no lounge access
- Amex Gold — no lounge access

**How to use Priority Pass:**
1. Register your card to receive Priority Pass membership card/app
2. Download Priority Pass app — shows all lounges by airport
3. At lounge: show your Priority Pass card or app + boarding pass
4. You're in — free food, drinks, WiFi, showers

### The Capital One Venture X Secret
At $395/year with a $300 travel credit effectively making it $95/year net cost, the Venture X gives:
- Priority Pass (you + 2 guests unlimited)

## TSA PreCheck — Get It Free

TSA PreCheck costs $78 for 5 years. You should never pay for it.

**Free with these credit cards:**
- Amex Platinum — $100 Global Entry credit (covers PreCheck too)
- Chase Sapphire Reserve — $100 Global Entry credit
- Capital One Venture X — $100 Global Entry credit
- Citi Prestige — $100 Global Entry credit
- Many airline co-branded cards — check your card benefits

**How to use the credit:**
1. Apply for Global Entry at cbp.gov (includes TSA PreCheck)
2. Pay the $100 fee
3. Submit for reimbursement through your card's travel credit portal
4. Most cards reimburse automatically when charge posts

**PreCheck vs. Global Entry:**
- PreCheck ($78): Fast security in the US
- Global Entry ($100): Fast re-entry into US from international + includes PreCheck
- **Always get Global Entry** — it costs $22 more and includes PreCheck + saves hours at customs

## CLEAR — Is It Worth It?

CLEAR ($189/year) uses biometrics to skip the ID check line entirely, then deposits you at the front of the TSA screening line (PreCheck or regular).

**Free with:**
- Delta SkyMiles status (Diamond gets free CLEAR)
- United MileagePlus status (some tiers)
- Amex Platinum — $189 CLEAR credit annually

**Worth it?** If you travel 15+ times per year through busy airports with CLEAR lanes (NYC, LAX, SFO, ATL, DEN, etc.) — yes, meaningful time savings. Less useful in smaller airports or off-peak travel times.

## Airport Navigation Power Moves

### The Real ID / Passport Reminder
As of 2025, standard state IDs without REAL ID compliance are not accepted for domestic flights. Ensure you have:
- REAL ID-compliant state license (has star in corner), OR
- Passport, OR
- Global Entry card, OR
- Military ID

### The Best Security Lines
1. **PreCheck** is almost always fastest
2. **CLEAR + PreCheck** is the fastest combination
3. If no PreCheck: look for families with children lane (often shorter, moves slower but more predictable)
4. Business/First class security lanes at many international airports — ask if available even if flying economy (some airports offer to status holders)

### Lounge Day Pass Strategy
Many lounges sell day passes at the door or via apps:
- LoungeBuddy app — book specific lounges in advance
- Priority Pass app — see which lounges offer day passes
- Often worth it for a long layover ($30-50 for unlimited food and drinks vs. $15-20 per airport restaurant item)

### The Overnight/Long Layover Playbook
For layovers over 4 hours:
1. Find the lounge for free food/drinks (use credits above)
2. Look for shower facilities — most international lounges have them, some Priority Pass lounges too
3. For overnight: Singapore, Dubai, Tokyo, Doha airports have exceptional transit hotels
4. Airside transit hotels (inside security): Minute Suites (US airports), Yotelair (international) — book via their apps

### Early/Late Flight Hacks
- **Early flights:** Hotel near airport the night before vs. waking up at 3am from home — often cheaper and far less stressful
- **Red-eye flights:** Choose window seat to lean against wall for sleep; bring noise canceling headphones and an eye mask; avoid alcohol (dehydrates)
- **Long layovers in hub cities:** Some airlines offer free layover tours — Icelandair (Reykjavik), Singapore Airlines (Singapore), Turkish Airlines (Istanbul). Ask when booking.
