# Mistake Fare Hunter

## Purpose
Teach users how to find, verify, and book mistake fares and flash sales — the unadvertised pricing errors and limited-time deals that can get you business class flights for economy prices.

## Trigger Phrases
- "How do I find mistake fares?"
- "Where do I find cheap flight deals?"
- "I saw a crazy cheap flight — is it real?"
- "How do flight deal alerts work?"
- "Find me the cheapest flights to [destination]"

## What Is a Mistake Fare?

A mistake fare occurs when airlines, OTAs, or booking systems accidentally publish a price far below intended — often due to:
- Currency conversion errors
- Missing fuel surcharge
- Incorrect fare class filed
- IT glitch
- Human data entry error

**Examples of real mistake fares:**
- Business class to Europe: $1,200 instead of $4,000+
- First class to Asia: $800 instead of $8,000+
- Round-trip economy to Australia: $300 instead of $1,200

## How to Verify If a Deal Is Real

### Step 1: Cross-Reference Immediately
Search the same route on Google Flights, Kayak, AND the airline's website directly. If you see the same price on 2+ platforms — it's real.

### Step 2: Check Historical Pricing
- Google Flights shows price history — compare to what the route normally costs
- If business class is showing at 30% of normal price, it's likely a mistake

### Step 3: Speed Is Everything
Mistake fares typically last **15 minutes to 4 hours** before airlines correct them. Stop verifying after 5 minutes — if it looks real, book it.

## The Booking Strategy for Mistake Fares

### Rule 1: Book First, Plan Later
Book the fare immediately. You can cancel most tickets within 24 hours for free (US DOT rule for flights to/from USA). Figure out hotels and plans after you've secured the ticket.

### Rule 2: Book Directly With the Airline When Possible
If a mistake fare appears on both an OTA and the airline directly:
- Book with the airline — higher chance they honor it
- OTAs may cancel your booking before the airline even sees it

### Rule 3: Book Multiple Passengers Separately
When booking for 2+ people, sometimes the mistake fare only has 1-2 seats available at that price. Book separately to ensure you both get the deal rather than being bumped to the next fare class.

### Rule 4: The 24-Hour Cancellation Window
All flights to/from the USA: airlines must allow free cancellation within 24 hours of booking, regardless of fare type. This gives you a free look to:
- Confirm the deal is real
- Research if dates work
- Check if airline honoring or canceling

### Will Airlines Honor Mistake Fares?

**Generally yes if:**
- Fare is published (not a glitch in your personal session)
- You have a booking confirmation number
- Tickets are already issued (look for 13-digit ticket number in your email)
- You booked with a major airline (Delta, United, AA, BA more likely to honor)

**Red flags they may not honor:**
- Price is >90% below normal (too good to be true territory)
- You receive no booking confirmation email
- Booking confirmation arrives without a ticket number
- Airline contacts you to "verify" the booking

**If they try to cancel:** Reply citing DOT regulations and your issued ticket number. Airlines that cancel issued tickets often face regulatory scrutiny and frequently reverse the decision when customers push back professionally.

## Flash Sales vs. Mistake Fares

**Flash Sales** (intentional, very short window):
- Airlines regularly run 24-48 hour sales
- Best times: Tuesdays and Wednesdays, especially in January and September
- Sign up for airline email lists to get notified
- Frontier, Spirit, Southwest, JetBlue run frequent flash sales

**Mistake Fares** (unintentional):
- Can appear any day, any time
- Usually much deeper discount
- More uncertain — may be canceled

## Cheap Flight Search Best Practices

### Google Flights Power User Tips
1. **Use the map view** — shows cheapest destinations from your city visually
2. **Enable price tracking** — get email alerts when specific routes drop
3. **Search with flexible dates** — the calendar view shows cheapest days at a glance
4. **Search nearby airports** — often shows dramatically cheaper alternatives
5. **Use "Explore" feature** — shows cheapest destinations if you're flexible on where to go

### The Positioning Flight Strategy
Sometimes flying to a different hub to catch your main flight is dramatically cheaper:
- Example: Flying Boston → London might be $900; flying Boston → New York (Amtrak $50) → London might be $450
- Check if deadheading to a hub airport saves money

### Best Times to Book
- **Domestic US:** 1-3 months before departure
- **International:** 2-6 months before departure
- **Holiday travel:** Book 4-6 months ahead minimum
- **Cheapest days to fly:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Saturday
- **Most expensive:** Friday, Sunday, Monday
- **Best booking day:** Studies vary but Tuesday/Wednesday releases often happen — check midweek
