# Credit Card Strategist

## Purpose
Build the optimal credit card stack for maximum travel rewards, identify the best signup bonuses right now, and tell users exactly which card to swipe for every purchase category.

## Trigger Phrases
- "Which credit card should I get for travel?"
- "What's the best signup bonus right now?"
- "Which card should I use for [groceries/dining/travel/gas]?"
- "Is [card name] worth the annual fee?"
- "How do I maximize my points earning?"
- "Should I cancel or keep my [card]?"

## What You Need From the User
1. **Current cards they hold**
2. **Credit score range** (approximate)
3. **Monthly spending categories** (dining, groceries, travel, gas, online shopping)
4. **Travel goals** (domestic, international, business class, hotels)
5. **Willingness to pay annual fees**

## The Optimal Card Stacks

### The "Best Overall" Stack (most people)
1. **Chase Sapphire Preferred** ($95/year) — 3x dining, 3x travel, transfers to best partners
2. **Chase Freedom Unlimited** (no fee) — 1.5x everything, feeds into Sapphire
3. **Chase Freedom Flex** (no fee) — 5x rotating categories (gas, groceries, Amazon)

*Why Chase first:* Chase has the "5/24 rule" — if you've opened 5+ cards in 24 months they deny you. Get Chase cards BEFORE Amex/Citi.

### The "Premium" Stack (frequent travelers)
1. **Amex Platinum** ($695/year) — 5x flights, $200 airline credit, Priority Pass, Global Entry credit, Fine Hotels program
2. **Amex Gold** ($250/year) — 4x dining, 4x US groceries — best everyday earning card
3. **Chase Sapphire Reserve** ($550/year) — 3x travel/dining, $300 travel credit, Priority Pass
4. **No-fee catch-all** — Citi Double Cash (2% everything) or Chase Freedom Unlimited

### The "No Annual Fee" Stack (beginners)
1. **Chase Freedom Unlimited** — 1.5x everything, 3x dining/drugstores
2. **Chase Freedom Flex** — 5x rotating categories
3. **Citi Double Cash** — 2% on everything (effective 2x ThankYou Points)
4. **Wells Fargo Autograph** — 3x gas, transit, streaming

## Signup Bonus Strategy

### How to Evaluate a Signup Bonus
Formula: **(Points × CPP) - Annual Fee = Net Value**
- Chase points: worth ~1.8-2.0 cents per point (CPP) when transferred
- Amex points: worth ~1.8-2.2 CPP when transferred
- Citi points: worth ~1.5-1.8 CPP when transferred

Example: Chase Sapphire Preferred 60,000 bonus
- 60,000 × $0.019 = $1,140 value
- Minus $95 annual fee = **$1,045 net first-year value**

### Best Times to Apply
- January: Cards often reset annual bonuses, new year offers
- Late spring: Summer travel pushes card companies to offer elevated bonuses
- Fall: Holiday spending bonuses frequently appear
- **Always check CardBenefit.com and Doctor of Credit** for elevated limited-time offers

### The Referral Bonus Hack
Always check if a friend or family member has the card you want — referring someone earns the existing cardholder 10,000-30,000 bonus points AND sometimes gets the new applicant a better offer than public links.

## Product Change Strategy (Never Cancel — Downgrade Instead)

When considering canceling a card:
1. **Never close a card** — hurts credit score (increases utilization, reduces average age)
2. **Product change instead** — call and ask to switch to a no-fee version
3. **Chase Sapphire → Freedom Flex or Freedom Unlimited** (keeps account open, no fee)
4. **Amex Platinum → Amex Green** (lower fee, still earns MR points)
5. Wait until AFTER the annual fee posts then call for retention offer before deciding

## Retention Offer Script
When annual fee posts and you're considering canceling:

*"Hi, my annual fee just posted and I'm evaluating whether to keep this card. I've been a customer for [X] years and spend about $[X] per month on it. What retention offers do you have available?"*

Companies frequently offer: statement credits ($50-200), bonus points (10,000-50,000), or fee waivers.

## 5/24 Rule and Application Strategy

**Chase 5/24:** Chase denies most applications if you've opened 5+ personal cards (any bank) in 24 months.

**Application order:**
1. Get all desired Chase cards FIRST
2. Then Amex (more lenient, but has own "once per lifetime" bonus rule)
3. Then Citi (24-month rule on same card family)
4. Capital One last (pulls all 3 bureaus — most aggressive)

**Amex "once per lifetime" rule:** You only get the signup bonus once per card ever. Don't apply for a card you've held before — you get approved but no bonus.
