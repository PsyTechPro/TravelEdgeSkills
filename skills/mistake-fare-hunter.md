# Mistake Fare Hunter

## Purpose
Teach users how to find, verify, and book mistake fares and flash sales — the unadvertised pricing errors and limited-time deals that can get you business class flights for economy prices.

## Trigger Phrases
- "How do I find mistake fares?"
- "Where do I find cheap flight deals?"
- "I saw a crazy cheap flight — is it real?"
- "How do flight deal alerts work?"
- "Find me the cheapest flights to [destination]"

## What Is a Mistake Fare?
A mistake fare occurs when airlines or booking systems accidentally publish a price far below intended — due to currency conversion errors, missing fuel surcharges, IT glitches, or human error.

Real examples: Business class to Europe for $1,200 instead of $4,000+. First class to Asia for $800 instead of $8,000+.

## How to Verify If a Deal Is Real
1. Search the same route on Google Flights, Kayak, AND the airline website
2. If you see the same price on 2+ platforms it is real
3. Check Google Flights price history to confirm how far below normal it is
4. Speed is everything — mistake fares last 15 minutes to 4 hours. Stop verifying after 5 minutes and book.

## The Booking Strategy

### Book First, Plan Later
Book immediately. You can cancel most tickets within 24 hours for free (US DOT rule for flights to/from USA). Figure out hotels after you secure the ticket.

### Book Directly With the Airline When Possible
Airlines are more likely to honor mistakes than OTAs, which may cancel your booking first.

### Book Passengers Separately for Groups
Sometimes only 1-2 seats are available at the mistake price. Book separately to ensure everyone gets the deal.

### Will Airlines Honor Mistake Fares?
Generally yes if:
- You have a confirmation number and issued ticket number (13 digits in your email)
- You booked with a major airline
- Price is not more than 90% below normal

If they try to cancel: cite DOT regulations and your issued ticket number. Airlines that cancel issued tickets face regulatory scrutiny and frequently reverse when customers push back.

## Flash Sales vs. Mistake Fares
Flash Sales (intentional, 24-48 hours): Best times are Tuesdays and Wednesdays, especially January and September. Sign up for airline email lists.

Mistake Fares (unintentional): Can appear any day, usually much deeper discount, more uncertain.

## Google Flights Power User Tips
1. Use map view — shows cheapest destinations from your city visually
2. Enable price tracking — get email alerts when routes drop
3. Search with flexible dates — calendar view shows cheapest days
4. Search nearby airports — often dramatically cheaper alternatives
5. Use Explore feature — shows cheapest destinations if flexible on where to go

## Best Times to Book
- Domestic US: 1-3 months before departure
- International: 2-6 months before departure
- Holiday travel: 4-6 months ahead minimum
- Cheapest days to fly: Tuesday, Wednesday, Saturday
- Most expensive: Friday, Sunday, Monday
