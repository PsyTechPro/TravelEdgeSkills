# Credit Card Strategist

## Purpose
Build the optimal credit card stack for maximum travel rewards, identify the best signup bonuses, and tell users exactly which card to swipe for every purchase category.

## Trigger Phrases
- "Which credit card should I get for travel?"
- "What's the best signup bonus right now?"
- "Which card should I use for groceries/dining/travel/gas?"
- "Is this card worth the annual fee?"
- "How do I maximize my points earning?"
- "Should I cancel or keep my card?"

## The Optimal Card Stacks

### Best Overall Stack (most people)
1. Chase Sapphire Preferred ($95/year) — 3x dining, 3x travel, transfers to best partners
2. Chase Freedom Unlimited (no fee) — 1.5x everything, feeds into Sapphire
3. Chase Freedom Flex (no fee) — 5x rotating categories

Get Chase cards FIRST due to the 5/24 rule — Chase denies applicants who have opened 5+ cards in 24 months.

### Premium Stack (frequent travelers)
1. Amex Platinum ($695/year) — 5x flights, $200 airline credit, Priority Pass, Global Entry credit
2. Amex Gold ($250/year) — 4x dining, 4x US groceries
3. Chase Sapphire Reserve ($550/year) — 3x travel/dining, $300 travel credit

### No Annual Fee Stack (beginners)
1. Chase Freedom Unlimited — 1.5x everything
2. Chase Freedom Flex — 5x rotating categories
3. Citi Double Cash — 2% on everything

## Signup Bonus Evaluation Formula
(Points x cents per point) - Annual Fee = Net Value

Example: 60,000 Chase bonus x $0.019 = $1,140 - $95 fee = $1,045 first year value

## Annual Fee Justification
Always calculate credits against the fee:
- Amex Platinum ($695): $200 airline + $200 Uber + $189 CLEAR + $100 Saks = $689 in credits before points
- Chase Sapphire Reserve ($550): $300 travel credit = effective $250 fee

## Never Cancel — Always Downgrade
Closing cards hurts your credit score. Instead:
- Call and request a product change to a no-fee version
- Chase Sapphire to Freedom Flex or Freedom Unlimited
- Ask for a retention offer before deciding — companies frequently offer $50-200 credits or bonus points

## Retention Offer Script
"Hi, my annual fee just posted and I am evaluating whether to keep this card. I have been a customer for X years. What retention offers do you have available?"

## Application Order
1. Chase cards first (5/24 rule)
2. Then Amex (once per lifetime bonus rule — never reapply for a card you have held before)
3. Then Citi
4. Capital One last (pulls all 3 credit bureaus)
