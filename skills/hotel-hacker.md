# Hotel Hacker

## Purpose
Help users get free nights, suite upgrades, late checkouts, and hidden perks that hotel loyalty programs and credit card benefits provide — most of which people never claim because they don't know they exist.

## Trigger Phrases
- "How do I get a free hotel upgrade?"
- "What's the best hotel points program?"
- "How do I use my points for hotels?"
- "How do I get hotel status?"
- "What perks am I entitled to at [hotel chain]?"
- "Is [hotel credit card] worth it?"

## Hotel Program Rankings (Best to Worst Value)

### Tier 1 — Exceptional Value
**World of Hyatt**
- Best redemption value in the industry (~2.0+ cents per point)
- Category 1-4 properties: extraordinary value (often $150-300/night rooms for 5,000-15,000 points)
- Chase transfers 1:1 — only major bank that transfers to Hyatt
- Globalist status (top tier): confirmed suite upgrades, club lounge access, free breakfast, guaranteed 4pm checkout
- **The move:** Transfer Chase points to Hyatt for luxury hotel redemptions

**IHG One Rewards**
- 4th night free on points bookings (Platinum members and above)
- Intercontinental properties often bookable for 40,000-50,000 points ($400-600/night rooms)
- Amex and Chase both transfer to IHG

### Tier 2 — Good Value With Strategy
**Marriott Bonvoy**
- Massive portfolio (30+ brands, 8,000+ properties)
- Value varies wildly — research individual properties carefully
- Titanium/Ambassador status has strong perks
- Peak/off-peak pricing means some properties are terrible value, others excellent
- 5th night free on points bookings

**Hilton Honors**
- Points worth less than Hyatt/IHG (~0.5 cents per point)
- BUT: Amex Platinum gives free Hilton Gold status (complimentary breakfast at many properties, room upgrades)
- Amex transfers to Hilton at 1:2 ratio
- Best play: use Amex Gold/Platinum status for free perks rather than burning many points

### Tier 3 — Situational Value
**Wyndham Rewards**
- Flat rate redemptions (7,500-30,000 points per night)
- Budget to mid-range properties
- Earns/burns well for road trips and domestic travel

**Choice Privileges**
- Good for extended stays
- Academy Award: the Cambria and Ascend collections punch above their weight

## Suite Upgrade Secrets

### The Check-In Time Strategy
- Call the hotel directly 24-48 hours before arrival
- Ask: *"I'm arriving for a special occasion — are there any complimentary upgrades available?"*
- Best time to call: Tuesday-Thursday mornings (when occupancy is being managed)
- Best time to arrive: Late afternoon (morning arrivals rarely get upgrades, afternoon has more flexibility)

### Status Match and Challenge Hacks
Most hotel chains will match status from competitors:
1. Achieve mid-tier status at one chain
2. Email competitor chain's elite services with your current status card
3. Request a status match or challenge
4. Chains that commonly match: IHG, Wyndham, Choice, Best Western
5. **Hyatt is the prize** — match to Hyatt Discoverist or Explorist and then work toward Globalist

### The Direct Booking Advantage
**Never book through Expedia, Hotels.com, or Booking.com for chains you care about:**
- Lose elite night credits
- Lose bonus points earning
- Lose upgrade eligibility
- Lose free breakfast benefits
- Hotels treat OTA bookings as lowest priority

**Always book direct OR through:**
- Amex Fine Hotels & Resorts (Platinum cardholders — daily breakfast, $100 credit, upgrades, late checkout)
- Chase Luxury Hotel & Resort Collection (Sapphire Reserve — similar perks to Amex FHR)
- Virtuoso (must book through a Virtuoso travel agent — includes perks at hundreds of luxury properties)

## Free Night Certificate Strategy

### Best Free Night Certificates
1. **World of Hyatt Credit Card** — annual free night at Category 1-4 property ($200-350 value, $95 annual fee card)
2. **IHG Premier Credit Card** — annual free night at almost any IHG property ($99 annual fee)
3. **Marriott Bonvoy Boundless** — annual free night up to 35,000 points ($95 annual fee)
4. **Hilton Surpass** — one free night after $15k spend ($150 annual fee)

**The math:** All of these free nights alone justify the annual fee — keep these cards forever even if you don't spend on them.

## Points Maximization Playbook

### Earning Points Faster
- **Shopping portals:** Use iDEALSHOPPING or hotel chain shopping portals for online purchases (2-5x points on normal shopping)
- **Dining programs:** Most chains have dining rewards programs — register your credit card to earn points at restaurants automatically
- **Mattress running:** When close to elite status, book one cheap night stay to push over the threshold if the perks of next tier are worth it

### The Points + Cash Hack
Many programs offer Points + Cash redemptions:
- Hyatt: Points + Cash often provides better per-point value than all-points redemptions at premium properties
- IHG: Cash + Points at $35 + 15,000 points sometimes beats using 40,000 points outright

## Script: Getting What You're Entitled To at Check-In

Say this at the front desk:
*"Hi, I'm [Name], checking in. I'm a [Gold/Platinum/Diamond] member — could you let me know what upgrades or amenities are available for my status today?"*

Don't ask IF you have benefits — ask WHAT is available. Assume you're entitled. Most front desk agents won't volunteer benefits unless asked directly.

**If denied an entitled benefit (breakfast, lounge, etc.):**
*"I want to make sure I understand correctly — [benefit] is listed as a [status tier] benefit. Could you double-check that for me? I'm happy to wait."*

Most of the time they'll correct themselves. If not, escalate to a manager — hotels would rather honor a benefit than lose a loyal customer.

## Airport Hotel Hack
Many airport hotels have day-use rates (4-8 hours) for ~50% of the overnight rate — great for:
- Long layovers
- Early morning flights (sleep at airport hotel, walk to terminal)
- Using a shower and working from a real space

Ask directly: *"Do you have any day-use rates available?"* Many hotels offer these but never advertise them.
